Having taken an early retirement from teaching, I am now pursuing my passion, which is web design and development. This past fall I enrolled in the Web Developer program at Boston University's Center for Digital Imaging Arts (CDIA) in Waltham, in order to take my self-taught skills to the next level. I decided it was time to learn all of the coding that went on behind the scenes when I used a program such as Dreamweaver.

Although just a few months into the program, my HTML and CSS skills have improved immensely. I have added JavaScript and jQuery knowledge, and I am very excited to expand my grasp of PHP and MySQL in approaching modules. The program will also cover eCommerce, AJAX, WordPress, and developing for mobile devices.

My design and development experience prior to beginning at CDIA came mainly from building and maintaining a site that tracks high school athletics in the Cape Ann League. CALScores (www.calscores.com) began as a simple spreadsheet tracking baseball in the league where I was coaching. Eventually it expanded to cover all sports, and then to the neighboring Northeastern Conference.

So now I am on to the next phase of my life, that of web designer/developer. I am looking to acquire real world experience as I finish my program at CDIA, with the ultimate goal of finding a position that will constantly challenge me to develop my skills, or becoming my own boss as a freelance designer/developer.
