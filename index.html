Developing Web Developer

with a passion for coding


Having taken an early retirement from teaching, I am now pursuing my passion, which is web design and development. I am approaching the end of the Web Developer program at Boston University's Center for Digital Imaging Arts (CDIA) in Waltham. I participated in this program in order to take my self-taught skills to the next level. I decided it was time to learn all of the coding that went on behind the scenes when I used a program such as Dreamweaver.

My HTML and CSS skills have improved immensely, especially with the addition of HTML5 and CSS3. I have added JavaScript and jQuery knowledge, including AJAX, and my skills with PHP and MySQL have increased exponentially. I have worked with WordPress, modifying and creating custom themes as well as building plug-ins. In our final modules, the program will be covering eCommerce, SEO, and developing for mobile devices.

My design and development experience prior to beginning at CDIA came mainly from building and maintaining a site that tracks high school athletics in the Cape Ann League. CALScores (www.calscores.com) began as a simple spreadsheet tracking baseball in the league where I was coaching. Eventually it expanded to cover all sports, and then to the neighboring Northeastern Conference. I have written an administration panel for the Athletic Directors, some of which are now using my site to do all of their scheduling.

So now I am on to the next phase of my life, that of web designer/developer. I am looking to acquire real world experience as I finish my program at CDIA, with the ultimate goal of finding a position that will constantly challenge me to develop my skills.
